稲荷神社.京都 Inari.Kyoto

October 14TH 。 Japan 。 Kyoto 。 Inari

Overview

We traveled from Tokyo to Kyoto via the Shinkansen, experiencing the convenience and speed of Japan's bullet train. The following day, we visited Nishi Hongan-ji and Higashi Hongan-ji, two significant temples that showcase stunning architecture and rich history. Each temple offered a glimpse into Japan's spiritual heritage, with serene gardens and traditional structures that left us in awe. Later, we explored Fushimi Inari Taisha, famous for its thousands of vibrant red torii gates winding up the mountain. This iconic shrine provided a unique cultural experience, allowing us to immerse ourselves in Japan's traditions while enjoying the beautiful landscapes surrounding Kyoto.

12:00 noon Lunch at 客美多

At 12:00 noon, we visited 客美多咖啡店 (Komeda's Coffee), a popular café known for its generous portion sizes at affordable prices. We decided to enjoy a meal that combined breakfast and lunch, sampling various dishes that showcased the café's reputation for value. The atmosphere was inviting, making it the perfect spot to relax and indulge in delicious food. The large servings and reasonable prices made our dining experience enjoyable, reinforcing why this café is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

12:50 PM At 西本願寺

At 12:50 PM, we hopped into an Uber and arrived at Nishi Hongan-ji. This historic temple, part of the Jodo Shinshu sect, is known for its stunning architecture and serene atmosphere. As we explored the grounds, we were reminded of the importance of following the temple's rules, which enhance the experience of visiting such a significant cultural site. The temple features beautiful gardens and traditional structures that reflect Japan’s rich heritage. Walking through this peaceful environment allowed us to appreciate the artistry and history that Nishi Hongan-ji embodies.

2:45 AM At 東本願寺

At 2:45 PM, we arrived at Higashi Hongan-ji, a significant temple in Kyoto that, like Nishi Hongan-ji, is part of the Jodo Shinshu sect. Established in 1602, this temple features stunning architecture and a peaceful atmosphere that reflects its rich history. We admired the intricate details of the buildings and explored the serene gardens, which provide a tranquil escape from the bustling city. The temple's cultural heritage and spiritual significance made our visit a memorable experience, allowing us to appreciate the beauty of traditional Japanese architecture.

3:15 PM Sweet Treats at Kyoto Station

At 3:15 PM, we enjoyed some delightful matcha sweets near Kyoto Station. This area is known for its high-quality matcha offerings, and we took the opportunity to indulge in a variety of treats. The rich flavors of matcha in desserts like parfaits or soft serve were a perfect way to recharge before our next adventure. With the calming taste of matcha, we prepared ourselves for the upcoming ride to Fushimi Inari Taisha, excited to explore more of Kyoto's cultural treasures.

5:15 PM Evening in 稲荷神社

At 5:15 PM, we arrived at Fushimi Inari Taisha, eager to explore its famous torii gates. As night approached, we realized that the temple lacked sufficient lighting, prompting us to start our descent before it got too dark. Despite the limited time, we found joy in our hike up the mountain, taking in the stunning views and the serene atmosphere. The beauty of the mountain, adorned with thousands of vermilion gates winding through the forest, created a magical experience that highlighted the unique charm of this iconic shrine.

7:15 PM Dinner at 本家第一旭本店

At 7:15 PM, after our Uber ride, we headed to 本家第一旭本店 (Honke Daiichiasahi), a popular ramen shop near Kyoto Station recommended by our driver. Upon arrival, we noticed a lively atmosphere filled with travelers eager to enjoy a hearty meal. Known for its flavorful soy sauce ramen and generous portions, this shop has garnered a reputation as a late-night dining spot. We were excited to join the crowd and experience the delicious ramen that many have praised, making it a perfect end to our day of exploration in Kyoto.
